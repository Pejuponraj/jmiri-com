Pearson Correlation

Understanding the linear relationship between two continuous variables

What is Pearson Correlation?

The Pearson correlation coefficient (r) measures the strength and direction of a linear relationship between two continuous variables.

Range: -1 (perfect negative) to +1 (perfect positive)

Calculate sums:

  • ΣX = 55
  • ΣY = 470
  • ΣXY = 2887
  • ΣX² = 385
  • ΣY² = 23198
  • n = 10

Substitute in the formula:

r = [nΣ(XY) - ΣXΣY] / sqrt{ [nΣX² - (ΣX)²] [nΣY² - (ΣY)²] }
r = [10 × 2887 - 55 × 470] / sqrt{ [10 × 385 - 55²] [10 × 23198 - 470²] }
r = (28870 - 25850) / sqrt{ (3850 - 3025) × (231980 - 220900) }
r = 3020 / sqrt(825 × 11080)
r ≈ 3020 / 3015.77 ≈ 1.00

This result indicates a nearly perfect positive correlation.

Interpretation

A Pearson correlation coefficient close to +1 indicates a strong positive linear relationship between years of experience and monthly salary. This means as experience increases, salary tends to increase almost perfectly linearly.

The p-value confirms statistical significance if it is less than 0.05.
